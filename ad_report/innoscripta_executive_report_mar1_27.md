In simple terms: for every €232 spent on ads, one potential customer submitted their contact information. For every €812 spent, one qualified meeting was booked. However, roughly 62% of appointment bookings were from companies too small to qualify (under 20 employees), which is inflating costs and represents a significant targeting opportunity.

## Obstacles We Identified, Flagged, and Resolved

Throughout this period, the ad performance numbers do not tell the full story. Behind the scenes, we uncovered and resolved a series of critical obstacles in the post-click experience and internal operations that were silently killing conversions. Many of these issues existed before our involvement and were directly responsible for the gap between ad clicks and booked appointments. Every issue listed below was identified by our team, flagged to the appropriate stakeholders — in many cases, multiple times — and has since been addressed or is actively being resolved.

### ✅ Resolved: The Booking Page Was Broken on Mobile

This was the single biggest conversion killer. The booking call page had catastrophic usability issues on mobile — which is where the majority of Facebook and Instagram ad traffic lands. The calendar widget was not appearing on screen for many visitors. For those where it did load, it was buried below the fold, completely hidden behind the salesperson's profile information and description, which consumed the entire visible screen. Visitors had no visual cue that they needed to scroll down to find a calendar. Many people literally could not book a call because they didn't know a calendar existed on the page. We identified this issue, flagged it, and it has since been corrected — the calendar is now visible and accessible above the fold on mobile devices.

### ✅ Resolved: CAPTCHA Was Driving Leads Away

A CAPTCHA verification was placed in the booking flow, and it was actively forcing qualified leads to abandon the process. People who had already clicked the ad, landed on the page, filled out their information, and were ready to book — were being hit with a friction-heavy CAPTCHA that many either failed, found annoying, or simply didn't want to deal with. This was essentially a wall placed right before the finish line. We flagged this as a critical conversion blocker, and it has been addressed.

### ✅ Resolved: Calendar Availability Was Misleading

Visitors who made it to the booking page were seeing extremely limited availability — in some cases, only one hour per day. This was not an accurate reflection of the sales team's actual schedule. We verified directly with the sales team that they had far more open slots than what was being displayed, but the calendar integration was not syncing properly and was showing a fraction of the real availability. Naturally, if someone couldn't find a time that worked for them, they left — and we lost the lead. We flagged this to the relevant stakeholders and worked with the team to ensure the calendar reflects actual availability.

### ⏳ Flagged, Yet to Be Resolved: No Automated Reminders for Booked Calls

The CRM currently has no automatic email or SMS reminders configured for booked appointments. This is standard practice in any lead generation operation — if you don't remind someone that they have a call, they will forget. There are no reminders sent at the time of booking confirmation, no reminder the day before, no reminder an hour before, and no reminder 15 minutes before the call. The predictable result: a significant number of no-shows. We have flagged this repeatedly and pushed for automated reminder sequences to be implemented across email and SMS at minimum — the day before, 1 hour before, and 15 minutes before the scheduled call. This remains unresolved and is actively costing qualified appointments.

### ⏳ Flagged, Yet to Be Resolved: Sales Team Has No Real-Time Lead Notifications

Speed-to-lead is one of the most critical factors in show-up rates and conversion. Industry research consistently shows that contacting a lead within the first 30 minutes to 1 hour dramatically increases the probability of a successful meeting. The sales team is currently not receiving real-time notifications when a new lead comes in. Email notifications are technically in place, but email is not a real-time alert — it gets buried in inboxes, goes to spam, or gets seen hours later. What is needed are on-screen push notifications — the kind that Zoom and Teams display — or mobile phone notifications that interrupt the workflow and demand immediate attention. Without these, the sales team often doesn't know when a lead arrives and can't follow up in a timely manner. Leads that sit for hours without contact go cold fast. We have flagged this issue and recommended implementing push notifications through a CRM mobile app or a webhook-based notification system that surfaces new leads immediately. This remains unresolved and is directly impacting show-up rates.

### ⏳ Flagged, Yet to Be Resolved: No Clear Ownership or Escalation Path for Issues

A systemic issue that compounded every single problem listed above: there is no clear hierarchy or ownership structure for who is responsible for what. When we identify a critical issue — like the broken mobile booking page, the CAPTCHA problem, or the calendar availability bug — we have to embark on a discovery process each time to find the right person, understand reporting lines, figure out who has the authority to make changes, and then wait for approvals from people we didn't initially know needed to be involved. This means that issues that could be fixed in a single day often take a week or more. There is no single point of contact, no defined escalation path, and no clear decision-making authority for marketing-related technical issues. Every obstacle in this report was flagged by our team — many of them multiple times — before action was taken. Establishing a clear point of contact with decision-making authority for marketing operations would dramatically reduce the time-to-resolution for issues that directly impact ad performance and ROI. This remains the single biggest operational bottleneck and is still unresolved.

## Bottom Line

This period saw a significant budget increase (more than 4x compared to Jan–Feb) but lead efficiency dropped sharply. The ads generated approximately 37 appointment bookings, but only 14 (38%) passed the qualification form — roughly 23 were rejected because the companies had fewer than 20 employees.

However, the declining numbers in the dashboard don't reflect the full picture. A significant portion of this period was spent identifying and resolving critical infrastructure issues — a broken mobile booking experience, a CAPTCHA that was blocking conversions, and misleading calendar availability. These weren't ad problems — they were operational problems that were silently destroying the ROI of every euro spent on advertising. These have now been fixed.

Three critical issues remain unresolved despite being flagged multiple times: no automated appointment reminders (causing no-shows), no real-time lead notifications for the sales team (causing slow follow-up and cold leads), and no clear ownership structure for resolving marketing-operations issues (causing unnecessary delays on every fix). These are actively suppressing performance and need to be addressed urgently.

On the creative side, the 750K Claims ad has emerged as a clear winner at €69.88/lead — nearly 3.3x more efficient than the account average — and is ready to scale. The path forward is clear: resolve the remaining operational blockers, scale what's working creatively, and continue refining audience targeting to reduce the 62% rejection rate. With these corrections, we expect to see a meaningful improvement in both lead volume and cost efficiency in the coming weeks.
