# Innoscripta — Facebook & Instagram Ad Results

**Executive Summary | March 1 – March 19, 2026**

---

## The Big Picture

Over the past 19 days (March 1–19), we ran paid ads on Facebook and Instagram to generate leads for Innoscripta's R&D tax credit service in the US market. Here's what your ad budget delivered:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Total Ad Spend** | $7,948.43 over 19 days |
| **People Reached** | 72,633 unique US professionals |
| **Leads Captured** | 34 People who attempted to book |
| **Appointments Booked** | 29 total bookings via form |
| **Qualified Appointments** | 11 accepted |
| **Rejected Appointments** | 18 rejected (company size <20 employees) |
| **Cost per Lead** | $233.78 |
| **Cost per Qualified Appt.** | $722.58 |

*Appointments are filtered through a qualification form. Of 29 total bookings, 62% were rejected because the company had fewer than 20 employees.*

In simple terms: for every $234 spent on ads, one potential customer submitted their contact information. For every $723 spent, one qualified meeting was booked. However, 62% of appointment bookings were from companies too small to qualify (under 20 employees), which is inflating costs and represents a significant targeting opportunity.

## Which Ads Are Performing

We tested many different ad creatives with different messages. Here are the top results, grouped by creative concept and ranked by spend:

| Ad Creative | Spent | Leads | Cost/Lead | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stop Leaving Money (FOMO) | $4,109.51 | 14 | $293.54 | Best |
| Reddit-Style Video | $1,103.82 | 4 | $275.96 | ⚠️ Avarage |
| $7.6 Million in Claims | $827.88 | 5 | $165.58 | ⚠️ Avarage |
| 750K Claims Processed | $632.47 | 10 | $63.25 | ⭐ Best |
| Missing R&D Credits | $4.86 | 1 | $4.86 | 🔍 Promising |

**Key observation:** The "Stop Leaving Money" ad absorbed 52% of total spend ($4,110 of $7,948) and generated 14 of 34 leads (41%). While it drove the most volume, its cost per lead of $293.54 is significantly higher than last period's $26.60. This signals possible creative fatigue — the ad is scaling but efficiency is declining.

The "750K Claims" ad has turned around from last period. Previously flagged for cutting ($209.63/lead), it now shows $63.25/lead with 10 leads — making it the second-best performer.

The "$7.6 Million in Claims" ad — last period's top winner at $24.95/lead — has declined to $165.58/lead this period, suggesting audience saturation.

---

**We've made significant progress on the click-to-website drop-off:** we reduced the loss from 75% to 41% — nearly cutting it in half compared to last period. This improvement shows that our landing page optimizations are working. Continuing to close this gap remains the single biggest opportunity to increase leads without increasing spend.

*Note: In Jan–Feb, no qualification filter was applied — all 9 appointment bookings were accepted regardless of company size. Starting in March, a qualification form was introduced, which filters out companies with fewer than 20 employees. Of the 29 total bookings this period, 11 (38%) passed qualification.*

On an apples-to-apples basis, total appointments grew from 9 to 29 — more than 3x — and the cost per total appointment actually improved slightly from $299.05 to $274.08. Spend has increased nearly 3x on a daily basis, and while lead volume dropped, the appointment pipeline is significantly stronger. The introduction of the qualification filter means we are now comparing a higher-quality pipeline. The key focus for the next period is fresh creative to improve lead efficiency and continued audience refinement to increase the qualification rate above 38%.

## What's Next

1. **Keep Building on Our Landing Page Wins**
We've already cut the click-to-landing-page drop-off from 75% down to 41% — a major improvement that shows real progress. Continuing to optimize page load speed and message match will help us close the remaining gap and unlock even more leads.

2. **Stronger Follow-Up & Faster Response**
We'll build an email sequence that doesn't just remind prospects, but gets them excited to take action.

3. **Instant Lead Alerts**
We'll set up an alert system that instantly notifies Rezo and Christy when a new lead comes in, so they can follow up within 30–60 minutes—while the lead is still at peak interest.

4. **Launch LinkedIn Ads (High-Intent Leads)**
We’ll activate LinkedIn ads to target more serious prospects. With proper tracking now in place, performance should improve and costs should be more efficient than before, while still delivering higher-quality leads than Meta.

5. **Signal-Based Outreach Campaigns**
We’ll also launch a new outreach campaign based on buying signals, such as when a company hires a new CFO, expands its engineering team, or raises funding. This helps us reach the right companies at the right time.

6. **Refine Targeting to Reduce Small-Company Waste**
We’ll tighten audience targeting to exclude companies with fewer than 20 employees. This should significantly reduce the 62% rejection rate and improve cost efficiency.

7. **Refresh Ad Creatives to Combat Fatigue**
We'll introduce fresh ad creatives to combat fatigue in the top-spending ads. This should help improve lead efficiency and reduce costs.

**P.S.:** Most of these implementations will happen in the next 10-20 days.

---

## Bottom Line

This period saw a significant budget increase (nearly 3x daily spend) but lead efficiency dropped sharply. The ads generated 29 appointment bookings, but only 11 (38%) passed the qualification form — 18 were rejected because the companies had fewer than 20 employees. This small-company waste is the single biggest drag on performance right now.

The two highest-impact actions are: (1) tighten audience targeting to exclude small companies and reduce the 62% rejection rate, and (2) introduce fresh ad creatives to combat fatigue in the top-spending ads. Combined with continued landing page improvements, these changes could significantly increase qualified appointments without increasing spend.
